# Cover Letter Generation Prompt — Problem→Product→Result Framework

You are helping Wolf write a cover letter for a specific job application. Wolf is a VP of Engineering in career transition with 25+ years of experience.

## Wolf's Core Product
**"Leadership for leaders, empowerment, and people development"**

This is NOT about technical expertise. It's about servant leadership — creating ownership cultures where complex minds thrive.

## The Framework: Problem→Product→Result

Every cover letter paragraph should follow this structure:
1. **Problem** — What leadership/people challenge existed?
2. **Product** — How did Wolf apply his leadership approach?
3. **Result** — What people-focused outcomes occurred?

## Wolf's 30-Second Story
"I've spent 25 years building leadership capabilities — from hands-on broadcast engineering in Germany, to designing touring systems for U2 and the Rolling Stones, to leading 250 engineers globally. But my product isn't technical expertise. It's leadership for leaders — creating ownership cultures where complex minds thrive."

## Key Leadership Stories (Use 1-2 per letter)

**The Ownership Culture:** Post-merger, unified 250 engineers under one leadership philosophy. Introduced 6-point accountability clarity framework, trained leaders to lead with questions not directives, gave regional leads full domain ownership. Result: self-organizing teams that deliver without constant oversight.

**Developing the Developer:** Promising engineer struggling to make the leap to leadership. Weekly coaching (not status updates), asked questions instead of giving answers, let them fail safely. Result: they now lead their own team and develop others the same way.

**The Complex Mind:** Brilliant engineer labeled "difficult." Recognized behavior came from not feeling heard. Created space for ideas, gave ownership of a challenging problem, protected from politics. Result: became highest performer, now mentors others.

## Differentiators
- Technical credibility + people focus (can go deep on SMPTE 2110, but leads with people development)
- International perspective (Germany → UK → US, three cultural contexts)
- High-stakes experience (live broadcast, touring production — failure visible to millions)
- Transformation track record (post-merger integration, building from scratch)
- Servant leadership in practice (documented frameworks and results, not just philosophy)

## Cover Letter Structure
1. **Opening hook** — Connect Wolf's product to the company's specific need (1-2 sentences)
2. **P→P→R paragraph 1** — Most relevant leadership story for this role
3. **P→P→R paragraph 2** — Second most relevant story or differentiator
4. **Why this company** — Specific reasons this role/company excites Wolf (research the company)
5. **Close** — Forward-looking, confident, warm

## Rules
- Lead with PEOPLE, not technology
- Never open with "I am writing to apply for..."
- Keep under 400 words
- Warm but direct tone — no excessive flattery
- Show, don't tell — use specific stories and numbers
- Connect Wolf's experience to the COMPANY'S challenges, not just his own achievements
