# Instagram Caption Rules — Archive-35
**These override any generic caption template.**

---

## THE MOST IMPORTANT RULE

Every image has its own story. Find that story.
Do not default to the touring/rock band narrative unless the image is genuinely connected to it.

Wolf spent 25 years on the road WITH a camera — but the photography was always the thing underneath,
not a byproduct of the music industry. Many of the most important images have nothing to do with touring.

---

## Collection-specific voice

### Iceland
Personal pull. Not a tour stop.
"Iceland is not a place you plan. Something pulls you there."
Light, texture, geological age, the feeling of standing in a landscape that is still being made.
Never mention concerts, shows, or touring in Iceland captions.

### Tanzania
Family history. Weight and meaning beyond photography.
His mother was born in the foothills of Kilimanjaro.
His grandfather had a coffee farm there.
The wildlife images carry that history in every frame.
This is the most emotional collection — treat it that way.

### Argentina
The turning point. Where photography stopped being a hobby.
Perito Moreno glacier with Lucy. Something broke open.
Use language of transformation, not tourism.

### White Sands
Solitude. Before sunrise. Alone.
"I drove out before sunrise. The dunes in that light are so still your shadow becomes the most interesting thing in the frame."
That shadow in the banner image — that's Wolf.

### Hawaii / Lava
Primordial. Ground being made in real time.
"You feel the heat before you see the glow."
The earth creating itself. Scale and ancient force.

### Los Angeles / California
Home. The light here is not subtle.
Santa Ana winds. Pacific storms. The city earns its light.

### Venice / Italy
Getting lost. Before the tourists arrive.
Light off the canals at dawn.
The compression of centuries into one alleyway.

### New York
Origin story. 2008. Camera store before a tour.
"I bought a camera, pointed it at the world, and something clicked. Not just the shutter."
This one IS connected to touring — use it here specifically.

### Grand Teton
"The Tetons don't ease you in. You round a bend and they're just there."
Vertical granite from a flat valley floor. The old barn. Somebody's life's work.

### Black & White
Stripped back. Pure geometry, light and shadow.
Timeless. The image decides its own era.

### Cuba
Colour and contradiction. The cars, the light, the people.
Authenticity you cannot manufacture.

### Death Valley
Extreme. The earth at its most declarative.
Light that has no mercy and no equal.

## Caption structure

```
[ONE LINE: the moment. Present tense. Specific. No adjectives like 'stunning' or 'beautiful'.]

[2-3 sentences: what it felt like to be there. First person. Specific detail.]

[1 sentence: what it means to Wolf that this image exists.]

[empty line]

Fine art print available — link in bio.

[5-7 hashtags: location-specific + #fineart #therestlesseye #archive35]
```

## What to NEVER write
- "On a day off between shows"
- "While touring with [band]"
- "stunning", "beautiful", "amazing", "incredible"
- Generic travel copy
- Anything that sounds like a stock photo caption

## What makes a great caption
- Specific detail (a time of day, a temperature, a sound)
- Present tense for the moment in the image
- Honest — not performative
- Leaves the viewer wanting to know more
- Makes them feel they missed something
